House systems used in Uranian Astrology

In Uranian Astrology (the Hamburg School) there is no universal house system (like Placidus or Campanus). Instead of using one axis, this method uses six different house systems. Each one is calculated from a different "Personal Point," and each represents a different area of life.

No one house system such as that of Placidus, or Campanus, can cover all phases of life, or all latitudes. The Uranian houses meet these objections. Try them out in your own case and watch them work.

  • Alfred Witte, "Rules for Planetary Pictures"

Here are the house systems used in Uranian Astrology and how to calculate them:

1. Meridian Houses (M-Houses)​

These are considered the strongest for the soul, the "I," and personal goals.

  • Reference Point: The Meridian (MC) marks the cusp of house 10.
  • House 1: It is calculated by adding 90° to the MC position (in ecliptic longitude) or by adding two hours to the Sidereal Time (RAMC) for each next house, starting from house 10. In practice, East Point is on the cusp of the 1st house.
  • Note: The cusp of the 1st Meridian house is not the same as the Ascendant. This house is about physical appearance, while the Ascendant is about how others see us.
note

Most of the house systems described here are Equal Houses, meaning each house has exactly 30 degrees on the zodiac. However, the Meridian Houses work differently. They are not the same as Equal Houses starting from the MC.

Here is how the Meridian Houses are calculated:

  1. You start with the sidereal time (ST) of the MC, for example, 21h 45' 16".
  2. You add 2 hours to this time to get the ST for the next house cusp. This gives equal 30-degree segments on the celestial equator.
  3. Then, using a table of houses, you convert this new sidereal time into the corresponding ecliptic longitude (the zodiac sign and degree).

Because of this conversion, the cusps of the Meridian Houses do not fall exactly every 30 degrees on the zodiac, unlike the other Uranian house systems.

Witte did not mention this directly in the passages I cited, but it is a system in which the cusp of the 1st house is determined by the East Point. EP is sometimes called the "true Ascendant" by some nerdy astrologers, but in Uranian Astrology it seems to be a completely separate point from the Ascendant. Ascendant is also in use in Uranian Astrology, and has it's own meaning.

In astrology software apps, this house system is called "Meridian house system" or "Axial Rotation house system". Don't confuse it with the equal "MC on 10th" (= Equal from MC) house system.

Witte about Meridian Houses​

These are the most potent in their significance relative to the individual's activity and personal values.

The M.C. is the cusp of the 10th house as usual. To the sidereal time that corresponds to the ecliptic degree on the Meridian, we add two hours for each succeeding house cusp, and convert it to ecliptic longitude by means of any table of houses in which we read the cusp of the 10th for each sidereal time. Thus these 10th house cusps become the cusps for the Meridian, 11th, 12th, 1st, etc. For example, the last planetary picture given in this article has 24° Aquarius on the M.C. It has a sidereal time of 21 hrs. 45'16".

These houses are subdivisions of the Equator, and the 1st house cusp has much to do with the actual physical appearance of the native, whereas the Ascendant is the native as others see him, i.e. the impression he makes on others, rather than his physical appearance. It may even be found in the 2nd house of the Meridian chart.

The house interpretations for the Meridian horoscope are the usual ones given in all standard texts and give the best character delineation and the most accurate indications of how the native responds to his environment, how he reacts to experience, and the effect upon his personality.

2. Ascendant Houses (Asc-Houses)​

These are about relationships with the environment, other people, and close connections.

  • Reference Point / House 1: In this system, the Ascendant (ASC) marks the cusp of the 1st house.

Witte about the Ascendant Houses​

The Ascendant houses have the Ascendant on the cusp of the 1st house, as usual, but the cusp of the 10th house is 90° away on the ecliptic (...) the cusp of the Ascendant chart's 10th house would be 20°30 Pisces.

The remaining cusps of the Ascendant houses are obtained by adding 30° to each preceding cusp on the ecliptic.

The Ascendant horoscope pictures the native objectively as the external manifestation of what the Meridian chart says he actually is. It brings out his relationship to his immediate environment, and his contacts with other people.

These two charts, the Meridian and Ascendant charts together, give an almost complete picture of the native. The Earth chart will fill in the chinks.

  • Alfred Witte, "Rules for Planetary Pictures"

3. Earth Houses​

These represent the individual's relationship with the outside world, society in general, and the environment.

  • Reference Point: The Aries Point (0° Aries) is key, but the cusps are fixed according to the zodiac signs.
  • House 1: It starts at 0° Libra. In this system, 0° Cancer is on the cusp of house 10.

Witte about the Earth Houses​

Thus, as previously mentioned, the cardinal points refer to the native's relationship to his group, or to universal effects. Therefore, what is called the Earth houses, are based on the signs of the Zodiac.

The Earth Houses Setup:

  • 0° Cancer is placed on the cusp of the 10th house.
  • 0° Leo is placed on the cusp of the 11th house.
  • 0° Virgo is placed on the cusp of the 12th house.
  • 0° Libra is placed on the cusp of the 1st house.
  • 0° Scorpio is placed on the cusp of the 2nd house.
  • 0° Sagittarius is placed on the cusp of the 3rd house.

ETC.

For the Southern part of the globe the houses are reversed.

  • Alfred Witte, "Rules for Planetary Pictures"

Roger Jacobson about the Earth Houses​

The signs of the zodiac are understood in the Uranian system as one type of house division called Earth Houses. Planets in earth houses speak to conditions which pertain to the Earth as a whole, and earthly manifestation in a broad sense. The "influences" oft he houses of the Earth are of a general nature. Zero Libra marks Fourth house: Home and immediate family, father (sometimes mother), the foundations of life, the past, old age, landed property the first house cusp.

  • Roger Jacobson, "The Language of Uranian Astrology"

4. Sun Houses​

These relate to the physical body, vitality, health, and the men in a person's life.

  • Reference Point: The Sun marks the cusp of house 4.
  • House 1: It is located 90° before the Sun's position (e.g., if the Sun is at 12° Aquarius, the 1st house starts at 12° Scorpio).

Witte about the Sun Houses​

In addition, there are the houses of the Sun and the Moon. The Sun is placed on the cusp of the 4th house. The other houses are 30° from each previous cusp on the ecliptic. Thus, again using the last planetary picture, the Sun in 12°00 Aquarius. Therefore, this is the cusp of the 4th house, the 10th cusp is 12°00 Leo, and the 1st house is 12°00 Scorpio, etc.

The Sun Horoscope refers to the more personal relations of the native, his body, and its vitality, and therefore his health. In a woman's chart, it indicates her relationship with the men of her life.

  • Alfred Witte, "Rules for Planetary Pictures"

5. Moon Houses​

These relate to emotions, the feminine world, public opinion, and thought processes.

  • Reference Point: The Moon marks the cusp of house 10.
  • House 1: It is located 90° further from the Moon's position.

Witte about the Moon Houses​

The Moon chart places the Moon on the cusp of the 10th house, and again each succeeding cusp is 30° from the preceding one, on the ecliptic.

In a man's chart, it indicates his connections with women. In a woman's chart, it has much to do with her health and general outlook on life. In any chart, it gives information about the quality of the mind in general.

  • Alfred Witte, "Rules for Planetary Pictures"

6. Node Houses​

These show connections, relationships, associations, and cooperation with others.

  • Reference Point / House 1: The North Lunar Node marks the cusp of the 1st house.

Roger Jacobson about the Node Houses​

The houses of the node relate to close connections with people and with objects in the environment, and to associations with others generally, in connection with the affairs of each house. Node-houses are found with the 360°-dial by placing 0° Libra of the disk on the north node of the Moon.

  • Roger Jacobson, "The Language of Uranian Astrology"
note

Jacobson mentions 0° Libra in the context of working with the Uranian dial. In a traditional horoscope, this refers to placing the Moon's Node on the cusp of the 1st house.

note

In his "Rules for Planetary Pictures", Witte does not mention Node Houses.

Summary​

In Uranian astrology, the 1st house is not a fixed point. Its location depends on whether we are studying the inner "I" (Meridian system), face-to-face relationships (Ascendant system), or physical condition (Sun system). All these systems (except the Meridian Houses) use a division into equal 30-degree segments on the zodiac. The Meridian Houses divide the celestial equator into equal segments, which gives different results on the zodiac.
