Ascendant and Vertex at extreme latitudes

I partially gave up on the idea of astrological houses, when cosmobiology and the 90 degree dial showed me that it's possible to do astrology without houses.

My doubts about houses started, when I saw their behavior at the North Pole and at the equator.

Using the Vertex-Antivertex axis in horoscopes calculated for the North Pole is a common idea. It should be noted, however, that this is not a universal solution. The Vertex, for instance, becomes unstable near the equator.

There are house systems based on the Vertex:

danger
  • "Vertex on 7th" in AstroGold: this is simply the vertex based counterpart for Equal House system where, instead of the Ascendant serving as the 1st house cusp, the Vertex serves as the 7th house cusp. Each house spans exactly 30°. It belongs to the same family as the standard Equal House or Whole Sign systems, only the starting point is simply different.
  • "Azimuthal Houses" in PlanetDance (= Zenith House System): this is a true spatial, quadrant system, belonging to the same family as Campanus or Regiomontanus. The Vertex is 7th house cusp here, and MC is 10th house cusp here.

Both systems utilize the Vertex but yield different results.

Below are two case studies about two extreme regions.

Case study 1: Longyearbyen, Norway (78°13'N, 15°39'E) and the ascendant​

  • Place: Longyearbyen, Norway (78°13'N, 15°39'E)
  • Date: 1 Jan 2026, 07:09:46
    • Note: The seconds are very important here, however, different applications calculate planetary positions slightly differently, and they may vary by a fraction of a second in order to achieve the perfect result, if you want to recreate this case.
  • Software: Astro Gold free version (MacOS), Planet Dance, Astro-seek, link to this particular example here
  • Observation: a conjunction of Ascendant and Midheaven: ASC at 01°30'51" Scorpio, MC at 01°30'40" Scorpio.

  • A second later, the ASC swaps 180 degrees, to 01°30'51" Taurus. MC stays in Scorpio.

  • Software behavior:
  • In AstroGold, quadrant houses crash. For Placidus and Koch houses the app automatically changes the house system to Porphyry, but it generates it (as expected) with errors, showing only 6 of 12 total houses. Campanus houses: the app shows only 2 houses (the 1st and the 7th).
note

At the North Pole, in quadrant house system based on ASC (Placidus, Koch, Campanus etc.) the 1st and the 10th house are overlapping.

  • In AstroGold, switching to Whole Sign or Equal system generates all 12 houses correctly.
  • In AstroGold, in theory, the equal "Vertex on 7th" house system should also work. But for some reason the app doesn't let you switch to this system in this case.
  • In Planet Dance, the Campanus houses show only 2 houses.
  • In PlanetDance, switching to Whole Sign or Equal system generates all 12 houses correctly.
  • In Planet Dance, the quadrant Azimuthal house system works correctly.

Case study 2: Pontianak, Indonesia (0°02'S, 109°20'E) and Vertex​

  • Place: Pontianak, Indonesia (0°02'S, 109°20'E)
  • Date: 1 Jan 2026, 16:58:05
    • Note: The seconds are very important here, however, different applications calculate planetary positions slightly differently, and they may vary by a fraction of a second in order to achieve the perfect result, if you want to recreate this case.
  • Software: Astro Gold free version (MacOS), Planet Dance, Astro-seek. Link to this particular example here
  • Observation: a conjunction of Vertex and Midheaven: Vertex at 29°54'58" Pisces, MC at 29°55'09" Pisces. This is a similar behavior, to the overlap of ASC and MC in Longyearbyen.

  • A second earlier, the Vertex was in a 180 degree opposite position, in 29°54'58" Virgo. It was in opposition to Midheaven.

  • Software behavior:
  • In AstroGold, for Placidus, Koch and Campanus houses (so the quadrant systems) the app generates the houses correctly.
  • In AstroGold switching to Whole Sign or Equal system generates all 12 houses correctly.
  • In AstroGold the equal "Vertex on 7th" house system works correctly.
  • In Planet Dance, Campanus house system works correctly.
  • In PlanetDance, switching to Whole Sign or Equal system generates all 12 houses correctly.
  • In Planet Dance, the quadrant Azimuthal houses crash. This is the same kind of crash that the ASC-based quadrant systems (Campanus, Placidus, Koch) had in Longyearbyen, just at the opposite latitude extreme.

Summary and observations about houses:​

  • For Longyearbyen:​

    • quadrant house systems based on ASC: don't work.
    • equal house systems based on ASC (Whole Sign, Equal, Morinus, Vehlow etc): work.
    • the equal "Vertex on 7th" should work, but probably there is a bug in the AstroGold app.
    • the quadrant Azimuthal houses: work.

  • For Pontianak:​

    • quadrant house systems based on ASC: work.
    • equal house systems based on ASC (Whole Sign, Equal, Morinus, Vehlow etc): work.
    • the equal "Vertex on 7th" from AstroGold "works" mechanically (it generates 12 houses with no error), but this is misleading: Vertex, just like ASC at the poles, is unstable at the equator. The whole ring of houses based on Vertex will jump by 180° at the exact same second when Vertex itself jumps. Probably this is not a fixed problem. It's a hidden error. The chart looks "complete" but it stands on a shaky point.
    • the quadrant Azimuthal houses: don't work.

Another serious problem: signs the Ascendant and Vertex can never touch​

The restriction on which signs can rise is well documented for the Ascendant, but not as clearly documented for the Vertex.

At polar latitudes, the Ascendant stays close to the equinoctial points (0° Aries and 0° Libra), because most of the ecliptic never crosses the horizon there: at 78°N, for example, the ASC can only move within about ±31° around these two points, and it moves in a retrograde direction near 0° Aries.

For the Vertex near the equator, the sources are less precise. I haven't found a source, that gives an exact degree range for the Vertex, the way it's given for the Ascendant at the poles. I should dig into this topic more.

Nevertheless, this behavior is a serious problem, even without the topic of houses. As I said at the start: astrology can work fine without houses. But it's still a problem, if these points can never reach certain signs of the zodiac.

MC doesn't depend on the observer's latitude at all: it's a function only of the sidereal time (RAMC) and the obliquity of the ecliptic. That's why MC never has a singularity, not at the poles, and not at the equator.
