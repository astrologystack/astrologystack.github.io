Ascendant and Vertex at extreme latitudes.

I partially gave up on the idea of astrological houses, when cosmobiology and the 90 degree dial showed me that it's possible to do astrology without houses.

My doubts about houses started, when I saw their behavior at the North Pole and at the equator.

Using the Vertex-Antivertex axis in horoscopes calculated for the North Pole is a common idea. It should be noted, however, that this is not a universal solution. The Vertex, for instance, becomes unstable near the equator.

Vertex-based houses seem to work regardless of latitude, but I suspect this is an illusion.

Vertex-based systems are not quadrant-based: they belong to the group of equal house systems. In these systems, each house spans a fixed 30 degrees: much like in Whole Sign, Equal House, Vehlow, or Morinus systems. The only difference is that the starting cusp is not the Ascendant, but rather the Vertex, which marks the cusp of the 7th house. So, Vertex based houses are the Vertex counterpart of Equal Houses counted from the Ascendant.

Below are two case studies about these two regions.

Case study 1: Longyearbyen, Norway (78°13'N, 15°39'E) and the ascendant​

  • Place: Longyearbyen, Norway (78°13'N, 15°39'E)
  • Date: 1 Jan 2026, 07:09:46
    • Note: The seconds are very important here.
  • Software: Astro Gold free version (MacOS), Planet Dance, Astro-seek, link to this particular example here
  • Observation: a conjunction of Ascendant and Midheaven: ASC at 01°30'51" Scorpio, MC at 01°30'40" Scorpio. Houses 1 and 10 are overlapping and in quadrant systems it's not possible to calculate them.

  • A second later, the ASC swaps 180 degree, to 01°30'51" Taurus. MC stays in Scorpio.

  • Software behavior: In AstroGold, for Placidus and Koch houses the app automatically changes the house system to Porphyry, but it generates it (as expected) with errors, showing only 6 of 12 total houses. Campanus houses: the app shows only 2 houses (the 1st and the 7th). Switching to Whole Sign or Equal system generates all 12 houses correctly.

  • In theory, the house system based on Vertex should also work. It's also called Azimuthal house system, or Horizontal house system. For some reason AstroGold doesn't let you switch to this system, but it worked in Planet Dance.

Behavior of Campanus houses for the Longyearbyen example, in the AstroGold app. House 1 and 7 are visible. ASC and MC are overlapping in Scorpio.

Behavior of Vertex based houses for the Longyearbyen example, in the Planet Dance app. The overlap of ASC and MC is visible in the upper part of the chart, Vertex marks house 7. All 12 houses are visible.

Case study 2: Pontianak, Indonesia (0°02'S, 109°20'E) and vertex​

  • Place: Pontianak, Indonesia (0°02'S, 109°20'E)
  • Date: 1 Jan 2026, 16:58:05
    • Note: The seconds are very important here.
  • Software: Astro Gold free version (MacOS), Planet Dance, Astro-seek. Link to this particular example here
  • Observation: a conjunction of Vertex and Midheaven: Vertex at 29°54'58" Pisces, MC at 29°55'09" Pisces. This is a similar behavior, to the overlap of ASC and MC in Longyearbyen. When I move the time one second back, the Vertex jumps by 180 degrees, to 29°54'58" Virgo, and the MC stays in Pisces.

A second earlier, the Vertex was in a 180 degree opposite position, in 29°54'58" Virgo. It was in opposition to Midheaven.

  • Software behavior: In AstroGold, for Placidus, Koch and Campanus houses (so the quadrant systems) the app generates the houses correctly, because Vertex is not used for them. Switching to Whole Sign or Equal system also generates all 12 houses correctly.

  • The Vertex based house system works correctly, in both AstroGold and Planet Dance.

Behavior of Campanus houses for the Pontianak example, in the AstroGold app. Vertex and MC are overlapping in Pisces. The houses are generated correctly.

Behavior of Vertex based houses for the Pontianak example, in the AstroGold app. The overlap of Vertex and MC is visible on the right side of the chart, Vertex marks house 7. All 12 houses are visible.

Summary and observations about houses:​

  • For Longyearbyen:​

    • quadrant house systems based on ASC: don't work.
    • equal house systems based on ASC (Whole Sign, Equal, Morinus, Vehlow etc): work.
    • Vertex based house system: works. Vertex at this latitude is stable.

  • For Pontianak:​

    • quadrant house systems based on ASC: work.
    • equal house systems based on ASC (Whole Sign, Equal, Morinus, Vehlow etc): work.
    • the Vertex based system also "works" mechanically, (it generates 12 houses with no error), but this is misleading: Vertex, just like ASC at the poles, is unstable at the equator. Generating 12 houses doesn't mean, they are meaningful. The whole ring of houses based on Vertex will jump by 180° at the exact same second when Vertex itself jumps. This is not a fixed problem. It's a problem that doesn't show itself as a missing cusp (like in Placidus, Koch or Campanus at Longyearbyen). Instead, it shows itself as a hidden error. The chart looks "complete" but it stands on a shaky point.

Another serious problem: signs the Ascendant and Vertex can never touch​

The restriction on which signs can rise is well documented for the Ascendant, but not as clearly documented for the Vertex.

At polar latitudes, the Ascendant stays close to the equinoctial points (0° Aries and 0° Libra), because most of the ecliptic never crosses the horizon there: at 78°N, for example, the ASC can only move within about ±31° around these two points, and it moves in a retrograde direction near 0° Aries.

For the Vertex near the equator, the sources are less precise. What is confirmed, is that the Vertex becomes less reliable and can swing between the 4th and 9th house instead of its usual 5th to 8th house range.

But I haven't found a source, that gives an exact degree range for the Vertex, the way it's given for the Ascendant at the poles. I should dig into this topic more.

Nevertheless, this behavior is a serious problem, even without the topic of houses. As I said at the start: astrology can work fine without houses. But it's still a problem, if these points can never reach certain signs of the zodiac.

MC doesn't depend on the observer's latitude at all: it's a function only of the sidereal time (RAMC) and the obliquity of the ecliptic. That's why MC never has a singularity, not at the poles, and not at the equator.
