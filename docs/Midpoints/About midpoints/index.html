About midpoints

What are midpoints?​

A midpoint is a point exactly in the middle between two planets in the chart.

For example: you have Sun at 10° Aries and Moon at 20° Aries. So, their midpoint is at 15° Aries. We write it as Sun/Moon.

In astrology this is a place where the energies of these two planets blend and create a particular meaning.

If a third planet (like Jupiter) makes an aspect to this midpoint (which means it is at the right angle to it) then this planet "hears" that mixed signal.

When we add the aspecting planet to the notation it looks like this:

Jupiter = Sun/Moon

We call this equation a "planetary picture".

The meaning, in the case of Jupiter = Sun/Moon and according to Ebertin, is:

A healthy mental and emotional state, a wish for joint effort, growth, and material gain. A happy relationship, shared success, marriage, shared happiness, birth.

How to find a midpoint meaning on this site?​

My digital garden will contain all possible combinations. To organize them somehow I created folders with names of the planets that aspect the midpoint (this is the part like Jupiter =). Inside each folder you will find a list of midpoints that this planet can aspect.

The structure looks like this, more or less:

Midpoints/
├── Ascendant/   <- all midpoints where Ascendant is the aspecting planet
│   ├── Ascendant = Sun/Moon
│   ├── Ascendant = Sun/Mercury
│   ├── Ascendant = Uranus/Pluto
│   └── ...
├── Jupiter/   <- this is where you find Jupiter = Sun/Moon
│   ├── Jupiter = Sun/Moon
│   ├── Jupiter = Sun/Mercury
│   └── ...
├── Mars/   <- and here Mars is the aspecting planet
│   ├── Mars = Sun/Moon
│   ├── Mars = Sun/Mercury
│   └── ...
├── Mercury/
├── Midheaven/
├── Moon/
└── ...

IMPORTANT: the list is alphabetical not astrological. Do not look here for the classical order (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars...). If I had to arrange it manually I would go crazy: there are 858 possible midpoints in cosmobiology and 5566 in Uranian Astrology 💀

Treat it like a dictionary: you know what you are looking for → you go in, you find it, you read it.

Could I have used a different layout?​

No. I use this files for my own research. In Obsidian (the note app I use for writing this site and my personal notes as well) I link to the relevant midpoints while writing chart interpretations. Clicking on a specific midpoint gives me a list of backlinks in Obsidian. I can see the situations in which a given midpoint appears. If I had created, say, a single long page containing all the midpoints for the Sun I would have lost this clarity.
